Epigastric (Supraumbilical) Hernia Repair

Home › Expertise › Epigastric (Supraumbilical) Hernia Repair

Overview

This hernia occurs where a small gap in the connective tissue running down the midline of the abdomen allows fatty tissue to protrude. It tends to enlarge gradually and can cause intermittent pinching pain, which is often the reason families seek assessment.

The Procedure

A brief day-case operation under general anaesthesia; the protruding fatty tissue is excised and the defect closed, with the wound covered using dissolvable sutures and surgical glue.

Preparing for Surgery

Routine day-surgery fasting guidelines apply.

Recovery

The glue dressing typically peels away on its own within about two weeks. Normal activity resumes within a few days.

Possible Complications

Low risk of infection, bleeding, or recurrence.

Follow-up

Review at three to four weeks.
