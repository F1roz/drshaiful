Wilms Tumour (Kidney Tumour) Surgery

Home › Expertise › Wilms Tumour (Kidney Tumour) Surgery

Overview

Wilms tumour typically presents as a painless abdominal mass, often noticed incidentally by a parent, and is one of the most curable childhood solid tumours when managed through coordinated multidisciplinary care.

The Procedure

Surgery generally involves removal of the affected kidney (nephrectomy) or, in select cases, a kidney-sparing partial resection, often performed after a course of chemotherapy to reduce tumour size, in line with established cooperative-group protocols.

Preparing for Surgery

Staging imaging and multidisciplinary planning precede surgery.

Recovery

Hospital stay and recovery depend on the extent of surgery performed; further treatment is guided by pathological findings.

Possible Complications

Risks relate to the extent of surgery and proximity to major abdominal vessels and organs, discussed individually.

Follow-up

Ongoing follow-up is coordinated with the paediatric oncology team, including monitoring of remaining kidney function.
