Hepatobiliary Surgery

Paediatric Hepatobiliary Surgery

Home › Expertise › Paediatric Hepatobiliary Surgery

Overview

This subspecialty covers conditions such as biliary atresia (a blockage of the bile ducts presenting in early infancy with persistent jaundice), choledochal cysts (abnormal dilatation of the bile duct), and gallstone disease in children. Early diagnosis is particularly important for biliary atresia, where timely surgery significantly affects long-term liver outcomes.

The Procedure

Surgical treatment ranges from laparoscopic cholecystectomy for gallstone disease to more complex reconstructive procedures such as portoenterostomy for biliary atresia or excision of a choledochal cyst with biliary reconstruction, tailored to the specific diagnosis.

Preparing for Surgery

Careful pre-operative imaging and liver function assessment guide surgical planning; fasting instructions are provided prior to admission.

Recovery

Recovery depends on the procedure performed; more complex reconstructive surgery involves a longer hospital stay and structured follow-up of liver function.

Possible Complications

Risks vary by procedure and are discussed individually; ongoing surveillance of liver health is important after major biliary reconstruction.

Follow-up

Regular follow-up with liver function monitoring is arranged according to the underlying condition.
