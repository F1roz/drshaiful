Branchial Anomalies (Cysts, Sinuses & Fistulae)

Home › Expertise › Branchial Anomalies (Cysts, Sinuses & Fistulae)

Overview

Branchial anomalies arise when structures involved in early face and neck development do not fully regress, leaving a cyst, sinus opening, or connecting tract along the side of the neck. They may present at birth or become noticeable later, sometimes only after an infection.

The Procedure

Complete surgical excision under general anaesthesia is the standard treatment, with the exact approach tailored to whether the anomaly is a simple cyst or a more complex tract.

Preparing for Surgery

Routine day-surgery or short-stay fasting guidelines apply.

Recovery

Recovery is generally straightforward, with wound care advice provided at discharge.

Possible Complications

General surgical risks apply, including a small chance of recurrence if a tract is incompletely excised, and rare risk to nearby nerves depending on anomaly location.

Follow-up

Review at two to three weeks, with pathology discussion where relevant.
