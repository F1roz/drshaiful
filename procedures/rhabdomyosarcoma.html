Rhabdomyosarcoma Surgery

Home › Expertise › Rhabdomyosarcoma Surgery

Overview

Rhabdomyosarcoma is the most common soft-tissue sarcoma of childhood, and can arise in the head and neck, limbs, trunk, or genitourinary tract. Treatment combines surgery with chemotherapy and, in many cases, radiotherapy, guided by multidisciplinary cooperative-group protocols.

The Procedure

Surgical goals include biopsy for diagnosis and, where feasible, complete resection with clear margins, timed in coordination with chemotherapy.

Preparing for Surgery

Detailed staging and multidisciplinary team review guide the surgical plan.

Recovery

Recovery and subsequent treatment are directed by the wider oncology team based on tumour location and response to treatment.

Possible Complications

Risks are specific to tumour location and are discussed individually with families.

Follow-up

Long-term multidisciplinary follow-up is arranged.
