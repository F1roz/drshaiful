Paediatric Urology

Home › Expertise › Paediatric Urology

Overview

Paediatric urology covers a broad range of conditions including vesicoureteric reflux (backward flow of urine toward the kidney), hypospadias (an abnormally positioned urethral opening), pelvi-ureteric junction obstruction, and other structural conditions of the kidney, bladder, and genitalia identified either before birth, in infancy, or in later childhood.

The Procedure

Treatment ranges from minimally invasive endoscopic techniques to open or laparoscopic reconstructive surgery, selected according to the specific diagnosis, its severity, and the child's age.

Preparing for Surgery

Pre-operative imaging (such as ultrasound or specialised urinary tract studies) guides planning; fasting instructions are provided before admission.

Recovery

Recovery varies by procedure; some operations are day-case, while reconstructive surgery may require a short hospital stay with catheter care.

Possible Complications

Risks vary by procedure and are discussed individually with families before surgery.

Follow-up

Follow-up typically includes clinical review and, where relevant, repeat imaging to confirm a good outcome.
