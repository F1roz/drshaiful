General Paediatric Surgery

Umbilical Hernia Repair

Home › Expertise › Umbilical Hernia Repair

Overview

An umbilical hernia is a soft bulge at the navel caused by incomplete closure of the abdominal wall opening through which the umbilical cord once passed. Most close spontaneously by around four years of age; surgery is considered for hernias that persist beyond this, are unusually large, or cause discomfort.

The Procedure

A short day-case operation under general anaesthesia, typically taking around twenty minutes, in which the defect is closed with dissolvable sutures.

Preparing for Surgery

Routine day-surgery fasting guidelines apply.

Recovery

Most children resume normal activity within a few days; heavy activity is avoided for two weeks.

Possible Complications

Low overall risk profile; a small chance of wound infection, bleeding, or recurrence.

Follow-up

Review at three to four weeks.
