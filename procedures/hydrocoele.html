General Paediatric Surgery

Hydrocoele Repair

Home › Expertise › Hydrocoele Repair

Overview

A hydrocoele is a painless fluid-filled swelling around the testis, arising from the same embryological passage responsible for inguinal hernias. It does not harm the testis itself. Many resolve spontaneously in the first two years of life, so surgery is generally deferred until around age two unless the hydrocoele is unusually tense or symptomatic.

The Procedure

Performed under general anaesthesia through a small groin incision, the operation closes the communicating passage and allows the fluid to be absorbed naturally.

Preparing for Surgery

Routine day-surgery fasting guidelines apply.

Recovery

Day-case surgery in the majority of children, with return to normal activities within a few days.

Possible Complications

A small risk exists of injury to the testicular blood supply (more common at repeat operations) or, rarely, the vas deferens. A hydrocoele can develop on the opposite side in a minority of children; this cannot reliably be predicted in advance.

Follow-up

Follow-up review at three to four weeks.
