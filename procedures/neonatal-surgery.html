Overview

Neonatal surgery covers a group of uncommon conditions present from birth that affect the airway, gut, or abdominal wall — such as abnormal connections between the food pipe and windpipe, bowel obstruction, and abdominal wall defects. These conditions may occur in isolation or alongside other congenital differences, and often require a prolonged hospital stay together with lifelong follow-up involving multiple specialists.

The Procedure

Surgical approach is individualised to the specific diagnosis, gestational age, and overall health of the newborn, and is planned in close coordination with neonatology and anaesthesia teams.

Preparing for Surgery

Newborns requiring surgery are cared for in a specialised neonatal unit with input from a multidisciplinary team before and after the operation.

Recovery

Recovery timelines vary considerably depending on the underlying condition and the baby's overall health, and are discussed individually with families.

Possible Complications

Risks are specific to each condition and are discussed in detail with parents prior to surgery.

Follow-up

Long-term, multidisciplinary follow-up is typically required.
